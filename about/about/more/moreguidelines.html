More Funding Guidelines

 

“The persistent question that served to guide our final selections was, 'Who are the multi-cultural representatives who are

  • currently bridging cultures, generations, or disciplines
  • making a positive impact in more than one community
  • committed to compassionate service and the upliftment of the human spirit.'"

      - Angie

“It was very important to us to give the Flow Fund money to organizations or individuals where it will have the greatest impact on the greatest number of individuals and will meet our goals of helping to change the horrific environmental conditions left by the former Soviet Union.  It is also important that we are ‘giving out fishing poles and teaching people how to fish’ instead of giving fish.  These goals mean that we have to help people be creative in their thinking (many people are used to old hierarchical thinking) and we must look long and hard before we find projects that fit our criteria.  This takes a lot of time – and reaching out to people we do not as yet know.”
- Enid Schreibman and Fran Macy

“Gifts are awarded to tribal peoples whose work or projects protect, steward or restore the environment.
Gifts are also made to non-native peoples doing such work or to individuals or groups working with cultural survival, education and reconciliation….”
      - Joan

“In the previous rounds of aid that I have distributed, I have focused on assisting those who were less fortunate.  Everyone in this part of Sumatra was hit hard by the tsunami, but some more so than others: women who lost their husbands and had many children
to support, children who lost their parents, people without other sources of assistance.  Along the lines of the triage philosophy, there are a number of people who will be ok, and others who will die. The most effective focus of our energies is on those who are teetering in between.

  • Sustainable Development: I like the concept of giving people a hand-up, rather than a hand-out: creating opportunities for them to build upon their talents.   Give them seed money that can be used to get back on  their feet to support themselves and their families into the future.
  • Education:  Providing school fees to enable children to continue their education, as well as supporting others to pursue higher education or career development that will help them gain the skills needed to attain their dreams for the future.
  • Healing Arts:  Art is important for the well being of the soul.  I'd like to invest in people's creative endeavors through music, arts n crafts, natural medicine, etc.
  • Projects that benefit the community as a whole, such as planting an abundance of fruit trees throughout the village that will nourish and be available for all to share, purchasing guitars that will circulate around and encourage music that uplifts the spirit, assuring sanitary living conditions to maintain people's health throughout transitionary periods.
  • It is important to give back to the Earth as well as humanity.  I want to support organizations and people who are working to protect and nurture the land.
  • Accountability:  Too often well meant contributions get diverted into deep, greedy pockets.  It is important that the money arrives into the hands of the people it is intended for, rather than getting caught up in bureaucracy or corruption.   In order accomplish this goal, I intend to work together with honest people of integrity who are looking out for the good of the whole.
  • Grassroots:  Support grassroots projects that come from and are for the people.   While we may have the best of intentions to help others across the world, unless we include the local people in the decision making process, we are not able to offer the most effective aid.   We have many innovative ideas, and so do the locals.  They understand what they need to heal and move on.  It is important to listen closely to them and work as a team to create projects that will be most beneficial to the people they affect.”

      - Anonymous
