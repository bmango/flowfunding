Lotus’s “16th Birthday Giving Account”

As she was turning sixteen, Lotus applied to be a volunteer counsellor at a summer camp for children who were living in refugee camps in Bosnia. Lotus had never done anything like this before.  She and her stepgrandmother, Betsy, a volunteer music teacher at the camp, held several fundraising activities in order to raise money for their airfare, for the cost of bringing Bosnian children to camp, and for art supplies.

To launch Lotus on her path of philanthropy and volunteer work, Betsy’s friends began a Flow Fund Giving Account so that Lotus and Betsy would have money to donate as opportunities arose in Bosnia. Betsy mentored Lotus in her flow funding in Bosnia. They will continue to be ‘partner flow funders’ and make joint decisions until Lotus gains experience as a philanthropist.

In the future, Lotus will be able to write checks from her account to make donations to the issues that matter to her.  Additional money will be added to this account annually, as Lotus’ community service and experience in philanthropy develops.

Laura’s 30th birthday

“I got the idea for my ‘birthday flow fund’ from a friend who had done it for his 50th birthday. He sent $50 to 50 friends.  I learned about it when I was 28 years old and planned to send $30 to 30 friends for my 30th birthday.  But I was so excited about the idea that I couldn’t wait – so I did it for my 29th birthday.  I also couldn’t limit it to 30 people.  I ended up sending a check for $30 to 60 people.  I asked them to give away my money to an organization or individual, and also to send a check of their own that matched or exceeded my gift.

I figured that my $3,600 would turn into at least $7,200 because people would match my check with their own money.  I also knew that many would exceed the match and give more. In fact, some people sent a check to each of the groups I recommended, so the money more than doubled.

Sometimes I think that if I did it now, perhaps I wouldn’t recommend any organizations so that people could really decide for themselves.  But some of the friends and family I sent it to had never really given money away, so they didn’t know where to begin.  I was surprised and touched that people took it so seriously. It was just $30!  But some people told me that thought about it for weeks. They felt pressure to pick just the right thing. 

When I began to write my letter, the first draft sounded like ‘the world is falling apart and we have an obligation to help’.  It had an urgent and judgmental tone, which isn’t what I wanted at all!  So a friend advised me to go for a walk and really get in touch with my own heart.   When I came back from that walk, I sat down – and the letter just poured out.”

Laura’s letter:

May 1, 2000

"Gratitude makes sense of our past, brings peace for today and creates a vision for tomorrow."
– Melody Beattie


Dear [Friend],

It is my birthday again and I’m feeling grateful. My gratitude is for the friends, family, teachers and mentors that grace my life. When I stop to consider the gifts I have received from you and others in my life, I feel tremendously blessed, lucky and even joyful. There is such a joy in being connected to inspirational people who are wise and creative and are doing the work of healing the planet and building healthy communities.  

These are juicy times. Flowers are blooming, romances are budding, babies are smiling. There are moments when I walk down the street in San Francisco, and catch a waft of jasmine. Time stops. My brain’s constant thinking and spinning stops. I breathe more deeply. I come into the present moment.

And with all the joy that is here to be experienced, there is also an ache in my chest. I ache for my friends who are dealing with cancer diagnoses and the loss of their loved ones. I ache for the children who are being abused by those who are supposed to be their caretakers. I ache for the ancient forests that are being clear-cut in the name of progress. I ache for the farmers in India who are committing suicide in mass numbers because they are becoming further and further indebted to multinational pesticide and seed companies with no hope of getting out from under. I ache for the families of the more than 2 million people who now reside in American prisons. And I ache for those prisoners.

What keeps me going when my small efforts to make this world a better place seem futile or overwhelming are the relationships I have with people who are so different from myself on the surface, and yet who share such a fundamental sameness at the core. And there is a peace and a stillness in that shared core—an essence—that feels so true and real.

It is this joy, along with the seriousness I feel about the challenges facing our planet, that I want to share with you this year for my birthday. This is my 29th, so I am entering my 30th year of life. I’ve enclosed a personal check for $30. For my birthday I’d like you to take a moment to be still and to listen to what makes your heart ache or sing. Then I ask you to take out your checkbook and write a check that matches (or exceeds, if you are able) my $30. And I ask you to send the checks to an organization you feel strives to make the changes you want to see made in the world or in your community.

I hope you will feel free to make this donation to any organization (or individual) you wish. If you need inspiration, check out the enclosed brochure that I helped put together a couple of years ago. I’m also including some information about three organizations that I work with, support financially, and care about very deeply.

Thank you for your friendship. Thank you for your support. Thank you for helping to make this birthday a time for me to express my gratitude by giving something to you and to the planet.

Love,
Laura
